Outlast

Outlast is a 2013 first-person survival horror video game developed and published by Red Barrels. The game revolves around a freelance investigative journalist, Miles Upshur, who decides to investigate a remote psychiatric hospital named Mount Massive Asylum, located deep in the mountains of Lake County, Colorado. The downloadable content Outlast: Whistleblower centers on Waylon Park, the man who led Miles there in the first place.

Outlast was released for Microsoft Windows on September 4, 2013, PlayStation 4 on February 4, 2014, and for Xbox One on June 19, 2014. Linux and OS X versions were later released on March 31, 2015. A Nintendo Switch version titled Outlast: Bundle of Terror was released in February 2018. Outlast received generally positive reviews, with praise for its atmosphere, horror elements, and gameplay. As of October 2016, the game has sold 4 million copies.As of May 2018, the whole series has sold 15 million copies. A sequel, Outlast 2, was released on April 25, 2017, while a prequel, The Outlast Trials, was released on May 18, 2023. The Murkoff Account, a comic book series set between Outlast and Outlast 2, was released from July 2016 to November 2017.

Sneak-Peek

In Outlast, the player assumes the role of investigative journalist Miles Upshur, as he navigates a dilapidated psychiatric hospital in Leadville, Colorado that is overrun by homicidal patients. The game is played from a first-person perspective and features some stealth gameplay mechanics.

The player can walk, run, crouch, jump, climb ladders and vault over objects. Unlike most games, however, the player does not have a visible health bar on the screen and is unable to attack enemies. The player must instead rely on stealth tactics such as hiding in lockers, sneaking past enemies, staying in the shadows, and hiding behind or under things to survive. Alternatively, the player can attempt to outrun their pursuer. If the player dies, the game will reset to the most recent checkpoint.

Outlast II

Outlast 2 is a 2017 first-person psychological survival horror video game developed and published by Red Barrels. A sequel to the 2013 video game Outlast, the game features a cameraman named Blake Langermann, along with his wife Lynn, a journalist roaming the Arizona desert to explore the murder of a pregnant woman only known as Jane Doe.

Both get separated in a helicopter crash, and Blake has to find his wife while traveling through a village inhabited by a deranged sect that believes the end of days are upon them.

The game was met with generally positive reception upon release, with praise going towards the graphics, sound design, and atmosphere. Criticisms were reserved for the amount of gore, and difficulty. A third installment in the series, entitled The Outlast Trials, was released on May 18, 2023.

Sneak-Peek

The game is designed to immerse players in a dark and disturbing atmosphere. The environments are eerie, with unsettling sounds and visuals that contribute to a feeling of constant tension and fear.

As players uncover the story, they encounter a twisted and mysterious plot involving a cult and dark supernatural forces. Themes of religion, violence, and psychological horror are prevalent throughout the narrative.

Graphics

The graphics in "Outlast 2" are highly detailed and realistic, immersing players in a world that feels grim, gritty, and disturbingly authentic. The environments, character models, and various elements are crafted to evoke a sense of unease and horror.

Outlast : Whistleblower

"Outlast: Whistleblower" is a DLC (Downloadable Content) expansion for the original "Outlast" game. It serves as a prequel and runs parallel to the events of the main game, providing players with additional context and insight into the sinister world of the Mount Massive Asylum.

The DLC puts players in the shoes of Waylon Park, a software engineer working at Mount Massive Asylum, the same setting as the main game. Waylon becomes aware of the unethical and inhumane experiments conducted at the asylum by the Murkoff Corporation. Fearing for his life and seeking to expose the truth, Waylon decides to leak information to the outside world, thus becoming a whistleblower.

Sneak-Peek

The DLC delves deeper into the events that occurred before and during the outbreak at Mount Massive Asylum. Players experience Waylon's journey as he attempts to escape the horrors unfolding within the facility while facing various dangers and threats.

"Whistleblower" sheds light on the dark secrets of the asylum and the insidious experiments conducted by the Murkoff Corporation, providing context to the events players encountered in the main game.

Graphics

The character models in the DLC are meticulously crafted, featuring grotesque enemies, disturbed inmates, and eerie figures. The design of these characters adds to the sense of fear and tension, making encounters with them more intense.

Chris Walker

Walker is a large, muscular man donning a straithjacket and exhibiting extreme aggression and violence. He's a patient who has undergone experimental procedures, turning him into a relentless and savage being. His pursuit of the protagonist, Miles Upshur, throughout the game creates a sense of constant danger and tension.

Richard Trager

Trager is portrayed as an employee of the Murkoff Corporation, the company behind the disturbing experiments and atrocities conducted at the asylum. At first, he presents himself as friendly and eager to assist the protagonist, Miles Upshur, in navigating the facility.

The Variant

The Variants exhibit extreme and disturbing behaviors, displaying signs of severe psychosis, violence, and physical deformities. They roam the asylum's halls, attacking anyone they encounter, including the protagonist, Miles Upshur.

These Variants are former patients who have undergone horrific experiments and treatments conducted by the Murkoff Corporation, resulting in their mental and physical degradation.

Nicholas Tremblay

Tremblay was a journalist investigating the strange occurrences in the remote region of Temple Gate, Arizona, where the game takes place. He was among several journalists looking into the activities of the secretive cult known as the Testament of the New Ezekiel.

Throughout the game, players can discover notes, recordings, and documents left behind by Tremblay, shedding light on the cult's practices, rituals, and the atrocities they committed. His investigations unravel the disturbing nature of the cult, its fanatical beliefs, and the horrors experienced by the inhabitants of Temple Gate

Marta

Marta is a member of the Testament of the New Ezekiel cult and is portrayed as a devout follower, wielding a large pickaxe-like weapon. She's deeply devoted to the cult's leader, Papa Knoth, and is tasked with enforcing their twisted beliefs and eliminating anyone perceived as a threat to the cult.

Sullivan Knoth

Knoth is portrayed as a deeply religious and authoritative figure within the cult, guiding his followers with zealous conviction. He espouses apocalyptic beliefs, claiming to receive divine revelations and visions that dictate the cult's actions and beliefs.

Knoth's character represents the embodiment of religious fanaticism, delusion, and the abuse of power.

Val

Val is portrayed as a high-ranking member of the cult, often referred to as "The Scalled" due to an illness that affected her and other followers, causing them to exhibit symptoms of skin lesions and sores. Val is deeply devoted to the cult's leader, Sullivan Knoth, and is involved in the propagation of the cult's apocalyptic beliefs.

Val's character serves as a representation of the fervent devotion and fanaticism present within the cult, contributing to the game's themes of religious extremism and the horrors resulting from extreme belief systems.

About Red Barrels

Red Barrels is the studio behind the immersive indie horror series Outlast, a series that has terrified more than 15 million players worldwide. Based in Montreal, Red Barrel's mission is to create unforgettable experiences that leave the player's blood running cold.

Red Barrels Inc. is a Canadian video game developer based in Montreal. The company was founded by Philippe Morin, David Chateauneuf and Hugo Dallaire in 2011. The three were previously developers for Ubisoft Montreal and EA Montreal, but founded the company after the origination of an original intellectual property by Dallaire in 2010. After leaving their respective workplaces, the team's online presence began in July 2012 and a trailer for the game that became Outlast.

Outlast: Trials

Outlast, the first major release from Red Barrels, is a first-person survival horror game that follows investigative journalist Miles Upshur as he explores Mount Massive Asylum, uncovering dark secrets and facing unimaginable horrors.

The game received critical acclaim for its chilling atmosphere, narrative, and innovative use of stealth gameplay in a horror setting. The Outlast Trials is a first-person psychological horror survival video game developed and published by Red Barrels.

Outlast Trials is the third installment in the Outlast series, serving as a prequel to the first two games and features test subjects in a mysterious Cold War experiment. The game was released on May 18, 2023 via early access for Microsoft Windows. Outlast Trials was announced in December 2017, though no time frame or target platforms were confirmed.[3] During this announcement, Red Barrels said that because they could not easily add downloadable content for Outlast 2 due to its structure, they have a smaller separate project related to Outlast that will release before Outlast Outlast Trials.
